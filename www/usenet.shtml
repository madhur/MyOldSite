Usenet

When I started on the Internet, the first thing I explored beside the WWW were chats. However, I didn't like chats very much because I sometimes had the impression that I tried to communicate with primates. So I found the Usenet newsgroups which I like a lot better. I think that's because a chat is a real time medium like the telephone, while Usenet is more like writing letters. You have time to think over your posting before you submit it, and the people you are communicating with hopefully do so as well :-)

I've tried to put together the most important things to pay attention to when posting on the Usenet.

- Don't do fullquoting (or top posting)

When answering a post, please don't just quote the full text and write your answer above it, it wastes bandwidth and is annoying because the postings grow bigger and bigger. But also don't quote nothing, it is important that you quote the parts of the texts you are referring to (but only them). A good quoting looks like the following:


Matthias Holzer wrote:

> Please don't fullquote.

I won't.

- Don't post binaries

Except some special groups (which usually carry the word "binary" in their name) binary files are unwanted on the Usenet. Some people download a whole newsgroup, and if there are binary files the amount of data may be much bigger than expected, and if they have only a telephone connection this can also be quite expensive. You'll get badly flamed for this.

- Read before posting

People can get quite angry if the same question that has been answered few days before is now asked again. So take a look at the postings that are already there before posting your problem. Also look for something like a FAQ list (list of frequently asked question and the answers), many newsgroup have one that is posted regularly, and you should not dare to ask something already answered there :-)

- Remember that your postings are archived

If you don't want your postings to be archived, you have to put the line "X-No-Archive: yes" in the header of your posting or in the first line of the body. But don't count on archives to look at this header. The biggest archive, Google, does, but there are other ones and some people even run their private archives. Also, if people answer you and quote your text it will still be archived. So be aware that what you post on Usenet is forever and there's no way to erase it :-)
